GOGROUP

GoGroup is a cross-platform web-app that uses reoccurring events to improve the meeting-making experience for college students. This six-month project involved full-stack development and close collaboration with two Stanford product designers and a Stanford software engineer. We employed the following human-centered research and design methods:

  • Mindmapping
  • User personas
  • Thumbnail sketches
  • Ethnographic research
  • Interviewing and storytelling
  • Divergent and convergent thinking
  • User roadmaps/storyboards
  • Information architecture
  • Rapid prototyping
  • Lofi and hifi wireframes
  • Mock-ups

USER RESEARCH

We spent four weeks interviewing and observing college students in different contexts to learn more about their meeting habits and needs. User interviews, mindmapping, and research tools like 2x2 matrices allowed us to robustly explore the many dimensions of our user's motivation. This culminated in the development of a strong point of view, which informed the next stages of the design process.

UX DESIGN

When designing the user experience, we needed to create an easy means of inputting a user's reoccurring schedule. This would allow us to accurately predict optimal meeting times for groups of our users. This solves a key painpoint identified through user interviews: competitors require the same information to be entered everytime a new meeting is created. One of the most challenging aspects of our design stemmed from the cross-platform experience. We knew users desired seamless connections between the desktop and mobile services. This informed clear, step-by-step instructions throughout a sequential sign-up process that would limit user dropoff. We achieved this by using unique links in text messages, which let the user continue the registration process with one click.

VISUAL DESIGN

I was individuallly responsible for the final product's visual design and most front-end development. I used DesignModo's flat user interface library of assets on top of Twitter's Bootstrap framework for the buttons, navigation bars,and form fields. I collaborated closely with our software engineer to connect the front-end and back-end.

I used Photoshop and Illustrator to create unique design assets like the sample text displayed on the home page and the GoGroup (and Plan.It) logos. Finally, I filmed, edited, and animated this short video early in our first iteration, when the project was still under the name Plan.It.
