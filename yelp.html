UX DESIGN

I was given a very open-ended task of re-designing the list feature, so my first goal was to narrow the scope. I developed two important design principles: making the solution visual and reducing the complexity of the interaction flow. Previously, users had to click through three pages in order to view or edit a list. My re-design consolidates all lists into one page, allowing users to locate a review easily. Yelp's power users have many lists, but are often looking for specific reveiws. This makes it more possible.

Yelp has many photographs for listings - especially popular reviews. Using these, I designed individual review cards that display a large thumbnail. More importantly, the cards - being significantly larger than the existing design - allow the user to see a portion of their review without ever clicking to another page. This reduces the amount of friction needed to locate a precise review.

VISUAL DESIGN

I created the hi-fidelity mock-ups using Photoshop and a template provided by Yelp. It was important that any modifications conform to Yelp's existing aesthetic.
